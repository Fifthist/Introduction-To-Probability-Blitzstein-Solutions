Let $A$ be the event that a man in the U.S. is a smoker. Let $B$ be the event that a an in the U.S. develops cancer.

\begin{flalign}
P(A|B) & = \frac{P(A)P(B|A)}{P(B)} \nonumber \\
& = \frac{P(A)P(B|A)}{P(A)P(B|A) + P(A^{c})P(B|A^{c})} \nonumber \\
& = \frac{P(A)P(B|A)}{P(A)P(B|A) + \frac{1}{23}P(A^{c})P(B|A)} \nonumber \\
& = \frac{0.22}{0.22 + 0.09} = 0.71 \nonumber
\end{flalign}
